05 / 06

Aerodynamics

Why air turbulence is one of the biggest weapons in F1 strategy.

💨
Clean Air

Running in open track, your car cuts through smooth, undisturbed air. Downforce is maximized, cornering speeds are higher, and tires behave predictably.

Max Downforce Stable Tires Faster Corners
🌀
Dirty Air

Following another car closely puts you in its turbulent wake. Downforce drops, corners get slower, and tires slide and overheat — erasing any speed advantage from new rubber.

–30% Downforce Tire Overheat Slower Corners
Why It Matters Strategically

Teams must decide: pit early for fresh tires and race in clean air — or stay out and hope the car ahead pits first. Dirty air can completely negate a tire compound advantage, making track position as important as pace.

What makes a good pit window?
  • At least 2–3 seconds gap behind — accounting for pit stop time loss (~22s)
  • Clean air to emerge into, not right behind a slower car
  • Strategic track position relative to rivals who are also pitting
